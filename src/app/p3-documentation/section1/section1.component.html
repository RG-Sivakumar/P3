1.0 Introduction Last updated:{{lastModifiedDate}}

Plannotate version 3 ("P3", "Plannotate") is the successor to Plannotate version 2 ("P2", "Plannotate2"), a software platform for the collection of field investigation and condition assessment data. Plannotate is a SaaS application for the Apple iPad iOS and computer web browser that interfaces with an internet accessible web server, otherwise known as the Plannotate Cloud.

Plannotate is designed for the efficient collection of field data. As such, it allows powerful annotation capabilities for PDF documents and photos using various annotation icons or drawing tools chosen from a series of custom toolbars. Each annotation can then contain a variety of data such as forms or photos. The collected data can be synced to the cloud to provide data back-up, access to data for multiple users, and near real-time opportunities to review from anywhere in the world.

The user guide was drafted using the iPad interface and actions. In cases where the web client and iPad interface or actions differ, there will be an indication highlighted in green notating the differences. Please refer to Section 9.2 in the FAQs for detailed information.

1.1 Installing Plannotate iPad Application

Plannotate can be installed as a native iOS application on any Apple supported iPad. In order to install the application, follow these steps on your iPad.

Note

External (non-WJE) users will not be able to downlod the application from within the Plannotate website, but rather will install the application from the Apple App Store for Business. Please contact your WJE project manager for more information.

  • Open the Safari browser on your iPad.
  • Navigate to the Plannotate web client website.
  • Select the Apple icon in the top right corner.
  • Select Install at the popup window.
  • Navigate to your iPad's Settings > General > Device Management.
  • Select Wiss, Janney, Elstner Associates, Inc.
  • Select Trust "Wiss, Janey, Elstner Associates, Inc."
  • Select Trust.
  • Select the Plannotate icon on your iPad's Home Screen to launch the application.

Note

If you have Plannotate 2 already installed on your iPad, or if you are updating to the latest release of Plannotate, you will likely have already trusted the application and can skip the applicable bullets above.

1.2 Sign In To Plannotate

Once Plannotate is launched, you will be presented with a sign in screen where you can provide your login credentials.

Note

  • For WJE employees, your Plannotate login credentials will be the same as your computer login credentials. For example, your email will be your username@wje.com email address and your password will be your network password.
  • For external users, you will enter the email address and password that has been provided to you when added as a user to the application.
  • Email Address – Your WJE email or guest email address.
  • Password – Your WJE network password or guest password. By selecting the icon to the right of the password text box, you can toggle viewing or hiding the password as you enter it.
  • Remember me? – By selecting this option, Plannotate will remember your email address for faster access after signing out.
  • Forgot password – This option will apply to external users that have been granted access to Plannotate. For WJE employees, if you cannot recall your network password, you will need to follow the IT policies and procedures to reset your password.
  • Sign In – Once your login credentials have been entered, select Sign In to enter the application.

1.3 Application Tutorials

Upon logging into Plannotate for the first time, you will be shown a series of tutorial screens. You can advance forward and backwards through the tutorial using the < and > icons on the left and right side of the screen.

Note

Press the Skip link at the bottom center of the screen at any time to close the tutorial.

1.4 Popup Notifications

Upon logging into Plannotate for the first time, you will be shown several popup windows requesting access to specific iPad functionality.

  • Plannotate3 requests access to your camera in order to take photos.
  • Plannotate3 requests access to your photos in order to access your camera roll

Warning

It is important that you hit OK to grant access to your device's camera and Allow Access to All Photos or Select Photos in order to utilize the media functions within Plannotate.
